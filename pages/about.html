How It Began

From Six Alpacas to a Full Herd

Margaret and David Henderson purchased their first six Huacaya alpacas in the spring of 2012. Margaret, a lifelong textile artist, fell in love with alpaca fiber's extraordinary softness, and David's agricultural background made the farm dream feel achievable.

Within two years, their herd had grown to 20 animals. By 2016, visitors were stopping by the farm so often that the Hendersons formalized their first guided tours. Today, their daughter Sophie — a certified master spinner — leads the fiber arts workshops that have become the farm's most beloved offering.

What We Stand For

Our Values

🌿

Every decision we make at the farm flows from these core beliefs.

Animal Welfare First

Our alpacas are family. They receive exceptional veterinary care, nutritious feed, and the freedom to roam their natural habitat.

Sustainable Practices

From chemical-free pastures to zero-waste fiber processing, every step we take is mindful of the planet.

Fiber Arts Education

We believe everyone should have the joy of creating with their hands. Our workshops are designed to be accessible, not intimidating.

Community Connection

We host school visits, veterans' programs, and therapeutic farm visits — because the farm should serve everyone.

Craft Excellence

Every skein of yarn, every woven blanket, every felted item that leaves the farm meets our rigorous quality standard.

Full Transparency

We show visitors every step of the fiber journey — from shearing to finished yarn. No secrets, no shortcuts.
